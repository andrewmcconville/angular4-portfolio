I had been searching for ever higher resolution satellite data when I stumbled upon a lidar scan of Milwaukee County. Having never worked with a lidar point cloud, I was excited to see if I could tease out individual structures from the more than 120 million point scan; below is my process.

Lidar Point Cloud

The lidar data was split into several small segments which needed to be combined.

QGIS

Each lidar point cloud segment was converted to a digital elevation model and combined in QGIS. Their height values were then normalized and the entire collection merged into one image to be exported.

3D Printing

I took a small detour to see if the lidar data could be 3D printed and if so, would the area’s features be recognizable.

Data Layers

The elevation model was imported into Photoshop to adjust scale and apply light denoising. A color layer was added based on elevation. A roads layer was added from the Wisconsin DOT to enhance their visibility. Finally, lidar intensity data was added as a final layer to add texture.

Rendering

The layers were then combined in Blender to produce a color image with lighting applied.

Detail

The final image was printed at 24”x36”. The rendered output features not only Milwaukee’s larger buildings and structures, but also has enough resolution to faithfully resolve individual houses and even trees.
