Hi, I’m Andrew McConville.
I'm a UX professional with a background in B2B SaaS applications.

UX Research & Design Philosophy

I believe a great user experience comes from uncovering a deep understanding your users needs and goals, along with adhering to the mantra: you are not your user. I’m also a believer in having all roles present during ideation: design, engineering, QA, PMs, marketing; bring them all to the design sprint.

I'm also a stickler for consistency and feel great UX design comes from adhering to a well documented and maintained design system. Teach users your design language once, then stay out of their way. Good UX should be invisible.

UX Process

I follow Design Thinking methodologies, and believe rigorous user research throughout the process is a requirement to delivering a truly useful product.

  1. Empathize & Discovery

    User interviews and analytics, combined with ethnographic analysis, are the foundation to building meaningful personas and experience maps. These tools allow for uncovering the needs and goals users have.

  2. Define & Hypothesize

    Understand the pain points and write a User Centered Problem Statement, then come back to it religiously to stay on track. Generate a research hypothesis and a metric to validate against to measure success.

  3. Ideate

    The best part of the design sprint: sketch new possibilities, diverge on radical ideas, affinity map a starting point. Converge on a possible best solution, which still might not be the solution.

  4. Prototype

    The time to "Move fast and break things." Build paper prototypes, sticky note prototypes, wireframe prototypes, or even working prototypes.

  5. Test & Validate

    Gather participants to click, tap, and reveal hidden flaws. Validate outcomes with metrics from survey data, behavioral and attitudinal research, desirability studies, and other relevant quantitive or qualitative metrics.
